Add Health analysis

Advisor: Dr. Nicole Legate

After being awarded the 2019 IIT Psychology Department Undergraduate Research Grant, I am working with a partner to analyze The National Longitudinal Study of Adolescent to Adult Health - in short, the dataset is called Add Health.

Add Health has interviewed a set of people for five 'waves,' beginning in their teenage years and repeating the interview once every decade to add a new 'wave' of data.

Using SPSS, we are looking for connections between foster care status in youth and development of chronic illness or 'unhealthy' eating habits in adulthood. We will also look for the same connection to chronic illness and dietary choices for American Indian Add Health interviewees - another vulnerable population.

To learn more about the dataset, visit the Add Health website here.

'Faces' study

Advisor: Dr. Nicole Legate

As two groups assigned in the PSYC204 course at Illinois Institute of Technology, we conducted a survey to understand how people form first impressions of others. Pictures of 120 faces, with each depicted race being represented an equal number of times in the set of pictures, were shown to the participants. They would rate reach face on a scale of 1-9 for a randomly-generated trait. There were a total of 13 traits - some were positive traits such as 'caring' or 'attractive,' while others were negative such as 'aggressive' or 'weird.' The ratings were done twice to ensure reliability of responses.

For example, a participant could see an image of a white person making an angry facial expression and be asked to rate the face on a scale of 1-9 on the trait 'emotionally stable.' We wanted to see if, overall, there are patterns in how people make first impressions based on the gender or race of the rater. Similarly, we wanted to see if there were patterns in formation of first impressions based upon the race or gender of the face being rated.
